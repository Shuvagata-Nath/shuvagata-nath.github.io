Braille-X
A Self-Reliant, Economic, Refreshable Mechanical Braille Display

Braille-X was developed to enhance accessibility for visually impaired students. The project focused on designing a reliable and cost-effective actuation mechanism to convert digital text into tactile Braille. Expertise in mechanical design, prototyping, and material selection was gained through this work, resulting in a device that improves the accessibility of educational tools.

A Novel FSAE Impact Attenuator

An impact attenuator was developed to improve vehicle safety by absorbing collision energy and reducing the impact on passengers. The design process involved detailed simulations to optimize the shape, material selection, and structure of the attenuator. Locally available materials were chosen to ensure cost-effectiveness and accessibility. The design was then subjected to rigorous experimental testing, using a Universal Testing Machine (UTM), to validate its performance under realistic conditions.
