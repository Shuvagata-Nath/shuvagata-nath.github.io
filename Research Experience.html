Current Research Project

Topic: Life Cycle Assessment and Environmental Impact of Different Bio-degradable Solid-State Batteries for the Applications of Wearable Electronics and Implantable Medical devices

Shuvagata Nath, Saraf Tanjeeb Ahmed, Dr. Md. Aman Uddin
Supervisor: Dr. Md. Aman Uddin, Assistant Professor, Dept. of Mechanical Engineering, BUET

  • Conducted a comprehensive study on biodegradable solid-state batteries, focusing on their life cycle assessment and environmental impact, particularly for applications in wearable electronics and implantable medical devices.
  • Evaluated the safety, durability, and energy storage capacity of solid-state batteries, emphasizing the potential of biodegradable materials to reduce electronic waste and improve sustainability.
  • Proposed and initiated the use of openLCA software to analyze the life cycle of these batteries, with the research being part of a course task in ME413, Energy and Environment.

Undergraduate Thesis

Numerical Modelling and Experimental Validation of Thermal Comfort Enhancement in Parked Car Interiors and Development of an Affordable Intelligent Cooling System.

Shuvagata Nath, Saraf Tanjeeb Ahmed, Dr. Md. Ehsan
Fluid Mechanics and Machineries Laboratory, Heat Engine Laboratory, Bangladesh University of Engineering and Technology (BUET).
Supervisor: Dr. Md. Ehsan, Professor, Dept. of Mechanical Engineering, BUET

  • Investigated solar heat transfer and thermal behavior in parked car interiors through a series of controlled experiments.
  • Developed a numerical model using ANSYS Fluent, based on an ASHRAE standard CAD model created in SolidWorks, to replicate experimental conditions and performed simulations to identify the optimal volumetric airflow rate for effective thermal mitigation.
  • Designed an intelligent Console Box device with smart controls, and developed practical, cost-effective strategies to lower cabin temperatures, advancing thermal management solutions for improved comfort and safety in vehicles.

Currently being prepared for submission to the 9th BSME International Conference on Thermal Engineering (ICTE) 2024.

Undergraduate Research Works

      Shuvagata Nath, Team Automaestro
       Material Testing Laboratory, Bangladesh University of Engineering and Technology (BUET).
  • Developed an innovative impact attenuator design using locally available materials and conducted numerical simulations to evaluate how impact affects its geometry, leading to a refined design.
